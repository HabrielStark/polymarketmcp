# Hermes-PM Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

Hermes-PM should feel like a precise local control room for prediction-market
research: editorial, technical, calm under pressure, and built from evidence
rather than hype. The look is a warm-black financial notebook crossed with a
small instrument panel: tactile paper, hairline rules, tabular data, chartreuse
approval signals, copper audit marks, and a restrained red risk accent.

## Logo Concept

The mark is a compact **H** built from two decision rails and a folded messenger
wing. It should read as: Hermes carries the signal; the rails enforce risk. The
center dot is the auditable decision point. Keep the mark flat, geometric, and
recognizable at small sizes.

## Colors

| Role | Hex |

| Ink background | `#11130F` |
| Deep panel | `#1C2018` |
| Paper | `#F3EFE3` |
| Muted text | `#B9B5A6` |
| Signal green | `#B7F34A` |
| Audit copper | `#C97B45` |
| Risk red | `#E85B4D` |

## Typography

- Display: `"Fraunces"` at 800-900 for editorial authority.
- Data and UI: `"IBM Plex Mono"` at 400-700 with tabular numerals.
- Avoid generic tech defaults and avoid pairing two sans-serif families.

## Motion Rules

- Use mechanical but graceful motion: rail wipes, ledger reveals, aperture
  masks, and stepped data counters.
- Entrances are precise and varied: scale, x-shift, y-shift, line growth, and
  number rolls; avoid same-direction repeated fades.
- Scene transitions should feel like moving through an audit trail: push slides,
  shutter/ledger wipes, and controlled blur crossfades.
- No crystals, neon particle fields, purple-blue gradients, random orbs, or
  generic futuristic dashboards.

## What NOT To Do

- Do not use floating particles, bokeh blobs, crystal motifs, or fantasy/sci-fi
  props.
- Do not use purple/blue neon as the dominant palette.
- Do not render fake profit promises or imply live-money automation is unlocked.
- Do not use vague abstract curves as the logo.
- Do not hide the paper-first / locked-live boundary.
